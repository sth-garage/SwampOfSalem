Root cause

All timing was tick-based. setInterval(tick, 2200) is a shared clock — every person’s thoughtTimer-- and speakCooldown-- decremented on the exact same 2200 ms boundary. Even with different stat values, people with the same thoughtStat would fire simultaneously. The result: 10 people thinking at once, mid-conversation pairs exchanging lines at the same tick.

Fix — real-time Date.now() per-person scheduling

Replaced all tick-count timers with absolute millisecond timestamps stored per person. Each person now tracks:

  • nextThoughtAt — ms timestamp when this person next generates a thought. Checked and updated in gameLoop() (~60 fps) so precision is Â±16 ms, not Â±2200 ms.
  • nextSpeakAt — ms timestamp when this person can next say a line. Checked in tick() via Date.now() >= p.nextSpeakAt.

Timing formulas

TimerFormulaRange (thoughtStat/socialStat = 1 → 10)
thoughtDelayMs(thoughtStat) 20000/stat × (0.5 + rnd×1.0) stat=1: 10–30 s  |  stat=5: 2–6 s  |  stat=10: 1–3 s
speakDelayMs(socialStat) 12000/stat × (0.45 + rnd×1.1) stat=1: 5–18 s  |  stat=5: 1–4 s  |  stat=10: 0.5–2 s

The wide variance (up to ×1.5× spread) ensures that even two people with identical stats diverge after the first update and never re-synchronise.

At spawn each person’s nextThoughtAt is seeded to Date.now() + random(1500, 19500) — an 18-second spread. No two people think at the same moment from the very start.
